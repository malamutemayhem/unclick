City unveils ten year transit expansion plan

By Jordan Rivers, 20 June 2026

The city council today announced a sweeping ten year plan to expand the metropolitan transit network.

The proposal adds three new rail lines, doubles weekend service frequency, and commits to a fully accessible fleet by 2032.

Officials said the plan would cut average commute times by a fifth and reduce road congestion in the central business district.

Funding will come from a mix of federal grants and a modest levy on commercial parking, the council said in a statement.

Community groups broadly welcomed the announcement while urging the council to protect existing bus routes during construction.
