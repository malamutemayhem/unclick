# UnClick Investor Deck - Speaker Notes

Plain-English talking points for each slide, plus what to fill in and what to
keep honest. The deck is 24 slides as an overview. For a fast pitch, use the
"Fast 12" cut listed at the end.

## Per-slide notes

1. **Cover.** Set the tone. "UnClick is the universal remote for AI." One
   install, a toolbox of 900+ real actions, and a memory that never forgets.

2. **The 30-second version.** The whole story on one slide. AI is brilliant but
   trapped in the chat box. UnClick is the layer that frees it: five parts -
   Tools, Memory, Connections, Autopilot, XPass.

3. **Why now.** Agents are everywhere; they all just learned one shared plug
   (MCP, like USB-C for AI); nobody owns the fully stocked hub behind that plug
   yet. That is the opening.

4. **Problem 1: amnesia.** AI forgets everything when the chat closes. The
   "catch-up tax" line lands the pain. Analogy: an assistant with amnesia every
   midnight.

5. **Problem 2: no hands.** It cannot touch your real tools without a developer
   hand-wiring each one. Analogy: a smartphone with no apps.

6. **Thesis.** "Agents are powerful, but homeless." UnClick gives them a home.
   This is the bridge from problem to solution.

7. **The solution.** One install = a memory plus 900+ hands, across 218
   services, no wiring. The big analogy: the operating-system / app-store moment
   for AI agents.

8. **How it works (MCP).** Keep this fast. One universal port; UnClick is the
   most stocked hub. New tools appear automatically.

9. **Adoption.** The "wow, that is easy" beat. Three clicks: email, pick your
   AI, paste one URL. No setup. Adoption friction is near zero.

10. **Tools - breadth.** 218 integrations, 914 tools, 20 categories. The brand
    wall shows recognisable names so the breadth is felt, not just stated.

11. **Tools - by category.** The chart proves depth across every area of life
    and work. Real counts from the live catalog.

12. **Tools - the depth (quality ladder).** Not just many tools, reliable ones.
    L1 works, L2 reliable, L3 remembers you, L4 taps you on the shoulder, L5
    shows its work. 154 at the top tier, 189 hardened.

13. **Memory.** Survives every session, model, and device. Six plain-English
    layers. The differentiator: your memories live in your database, not ours.

14. **Connections.** Sign in once, the AI uses everything else, without ever
    seeing your raw passwords. Analogy: a building pass, not a copy of every key.

15. **Autopilot - the fleet.** When one AI is not enough: Orchestrator (your
    chat is the boss), Job Board (work handed to the right worker, nothing ships
    without a receipt), Boardroom (the shared team room). Construction-site
    analogy.

16. **Autopilot - wake.** It can wake itself on events and schedules, then
    report what worked, failed, or needs you. "Autonomy without observability is
    a blocker, not a feature."

17. **XPass - proof.** A roadworthy checklist for AI work before it ships, with
    one combined receipt. Trust is the wedge into businesses.

18. **The moat.** We are not another brain; we are the rails every brain plugs
    into ("Stripe for agents"). Three compounding advantages: the whole stack in
    one install, memory that compounds, and proof plus a learning loop. Plus the
    MCP tailwind, and zero switching cost to start.

19. **Versus AI in isolation.** The differentiation slide. A lone chatbot
    forgets, cannot act, works solo, and cannot prove its work. With UnClick it
    remembers, acts across 914 tools, runs a team, and ships a proof receipt. We
    make Claude, ChatGPT, and OpenAI better; we are not competing with them.

20. **Market.** Every company will run on agents; agents run on infrastructure.
    "We sell the picks and shovels for the agent gold rush." Keep it grounded:
    millions of developers, every agent needs memory + tools + identity + proof.

21. **Business model.** Simple subscriptions (Free / $29 Pro / $79 Team /
    Enterprise). We never mark up the user's AI. A tools marketplace (developers
    earn 80%) is the next layer.

22. **Traction.** Make the point that it is real and shipping: live remote MCP,
    three-click install, the full stack shipped, 1,000+ PRs built by a human +
    AI fleet running on UnClick itself, every change gated by UnClick's own
    proof receipt. (Add business metrics here when you have them.)

23. **Team.** Chris Byrne, Melbourne, designer-led, non-coder-first bar. The
    company runs on its own product, so it is its own first proof. (Add a photo,
    a one-line bio, and any advisors.)

24. **Close / the ask.** "Let your AI stop pretending to be human." Fill in the
    ask: amount, the milestone it buys, and the runway.

## Fill these in before sending to investors

- Slide 22: real business metrics (installs, active agents, calls per day, paying customers).
- Slide 23: founder photo, one-line bio, advisors or early hires.
- Slide 24: the ask (how much, what it buys, how long it lasts).

## Honesty guardrails (so the deck survives a sharp investor)

- **Numbers.** Use 218 integrations / 914 tools - these are verified from the
  live catalog (`src/data/app-catalog.generated.json`). The public site still
  shows the older "450+ / 178+" figures; they undercount and are being updated.
  Pick one set of numbers and stay consistent.
- **Quality tiers.** 154 tools at the top L5 tier and 189 hardened are real
  counts. Fine to say "the vast majority are hardened."
- **XPass.** Describe it as a proof and quality layer. Do not claim it
  "certifies" security, legality, SEO, or production readiness.
- **Marketplace.** The 80/20 developer marketplace is built but "coming soon,"
  not live revenue. Present it as roadmap.
- **Traction is product maturity, not financials.** There are no revenue or user
  numbers yet. The strength is what is shipped and the build velocity. Only put
  real numbers in the traction placeholder.
- **Autopilot scope.** Memory, Build Desk, Crews, and Signals are shipped. The
  fuller Autopilot / Launchpad / Rooms vision is partly aspirational; present it
  as direction, not all live today.
- **Framing.** "Operating system / universal remote for AI" is the friendly
  metaphor for a general audience. The precise, defensible framing for a sharp
  investor is "Stripe for agents - infrastructure, not a model or an OS."
